Virtual Science Center - Reinventing Reality

Reinventing Reality is a travelling exhibit that teaches visitors about the concepts and technologies that make virtual reality work. It has several VR activities as well as an interactive video wall, an eye-tracking game, and a spatialized audio game.

I started on this project as an intern. I programmed several prototypes and took observations during visitor testing. Later in the project, as the producer, I coordinated the software development, UI design, and creation of custom electronics. I also hired and supervised several freelance artists and voice actors. During the first installation, I travelled to the venue to install equipment and train the museum staff.

Reinventing Reality won the 2021 Gold MUSE Award from the American Alliance of Museums in the AR, VR, and Mixed Reality category.

Annapolis Maritime Museum

This exhibit teaches visitors about the industry around the Chesapeake Bay. It has projections, listening stations, a virtual reality 360 video player, and a multi-player touchscreen game.

As the multimedia manager, I supervised the software development and UI design. I also worked on the installation, technical documentation, and support.

National Flag Foundation

This exhibit uses video, an interactive timeline, and visitor-submitted content to teach visitors about the meaning and history of the American flag. The exhibit also has a photobooth and a mobile app.

As the multimedia manager, I supervised the software development and UI design. I also coordinated between our team, the client, and the content producers by hosting meetings, distributing notes, and keeping tracking of deadlines.

National Aquarium - Maryland: Mountains to the Sea

This project added eight interactive graphics to an existing exhibit. I programmed an Arduino-based system that uses touch sensors and addressable LED strands. When a visitor touches one of marked areas, it triggers a sequence of lights that illuminate hidden images in the graphic.
